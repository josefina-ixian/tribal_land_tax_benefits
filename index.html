Sovereign Finance Guide

Cash for Clean Energy:
A Guide for Tribal Nations

Tribal Nations can now stack multiple federal incentives to offset 30% to 70% of project costs. These benefits flow from three primary statutes: (1) the Inflation Reduction Act of 2022 (IRA), (2) the One Big Beautiful Bill Act of 2025 (OBBBA), and (3) Title XXVI of the Energy Policy Act, as amended.

New to Tribal Nuclear Benefits?

1

Direct Pay

The IRS sends a cash refund check directly to the Tribe, covering up to 70% of construction costs.

2

The "5MW Line"

Small reactors (< 5 MW) qualify for the Indian Land bonus. Larger projects rely on Domestic Content and Energy Community adders.

3

DOE Interest Savings

DOE loan guarantees lower borrowing costs vs. commercial rates, creating massive savings over time.

How the "Stack" Works

Think of these benefits like building blocks. You start with a **30% Base Discount** (if using fair labor rules). Then, you add blocks for things like:

  • +10% Using American-made parts (**Domestic Content**)
  • +10% Building on former mining/fossil sites (**Energy Community**)
  • +10% Siting on Tribal Land (**Indian Land Bonus** - Micro only)

Combined federal support can exceed 70% of overnight capital.

Compliance Rules

FEOC Restrictions

Foreign Entity of Concern (FEOC) rules prohibit federal credits for projects tied to China, Russia, North Korea, or Iran. Tribal projects require strict supply chain due diligence for financing close.

Labor Standards (PWA)

Paying prevailing wages and using registered apprentices is mandatory for the full 30% base credit. Failure to comply reduces the credit to only 6%.

Critical Deadlines

2026 Bonus Window

The Section 48E(h) Indian Land bonus window for microreactors opens **Feb 2, 2026** and closes **Aug 7, 2026**. This is a competitive annual allocation.

OBBBA Phasedown

Credits retain 100% value through 2033. Projects starting in 2034 receive 75% value, dropping to 50% in 2035 and 0% for construction starting in 2036+.
