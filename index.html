The Three Statutory Pillars of Tribal Finance

1. Inflation Reduction Act (IRA)

Established **Elective Pay (Direct Pay)**, turning tax credits into cash grants for Tribes. Unlocked §48E Investment Tax Credits.

Source: IRS Elective Pay Guidance

2. OBBB Act (2025)

Extended nuclear credit values through **2033** and introduced Foreign Entity of Concern (FEOC) supply chain restrictions.

Source: OBBBA Analysis (July 2025)

3. Title XXVI (EPAct)

The **Tribal Energy Financing Program (TEFP)**. Provides loan guarantees for up to **80%** of project costs via the Office of Energy Dominance Financing.

Source: DOE EDF / Title XXVI FAQs

Special Benefits for Tribal Lands

The "Uranium Legacy" Bonus

Many Tribal lands host historic uranium mine sites (96% of all US defense mines). These sites often pre-qualify as **Energy Communities (Brownfields)**, adding an automatic **+10%** to the ITC stack.

Source: DOE/NETL Energy Community Mapping Tool

Indian Land Bonus (§48E(h))

Reserved for projects **under 5 MW (Microreactors)**. Adds an additional **+10%** for siting on Indian Land, and potentially **+20%** for Low-Income Benefit.

Source: IRS Rev. Proc. 2025-11 Size Constraints

Visualizing the Subsidy Stack

Effective stacks are capped at a 70% statutory ceiling. Most utility-scale SMRs land between 40-50%.

Compliance & Deployment Roadmap

1

FEOC Restrictions (2026+)

Projects cannot receive "Material Assistance" from Foreign Entities of Concern (Russia/China). Supply chain audit is now a pre-condition for Direct Pay.

Source: OBBBA Section 13105
2

2034 Phasedown Date

OBBBA (2025) extends full credit value through Dec 31, 2033. Projects starting construction in 2034 receive only 75% value.

Source: Morgan Lewis July 2025 Summary

Executive Takeaway

"For a Tribal government, the combination of a 50% cash refund (Direct Pay) and an 80% loan guarantee (Title XXVI) effectively de-risks nuclear deployment. The primary hurdles are now site qualification (Brownfield Energy Community) and FEOC-compliant supply chains."
