1. What are the Benefits?

Historically, clean energy tax credits were only useful to large corporations with tax bills. The IRA changed this for Tribal Governments through **Elective Pay (Direct Pay)**.

● The Cash Refund (Direct Pay)

Instead of a tax write-off, the IRS sends a **cash payment** to the Tribal government for the value of the credit. This covers 30% to 70% of construction costs.

IRS: Elective Pay & Transferability FAQ

● Energy Community Bonus

If the land was historically tied to coal or fossil fuels (common for many Tribes), the project gets an **extra 10%** bonus on top of the base credit.

IRS: Energy Community Bonus Guidance

Total Federal Support Potential

*Note the 70% statutory cap for micro-projects. Most large projects cap at 50%.*

2. The "Must-Follow" Rules

Labor Rules (PWA)

You must pay **Prevailing Wages** and use **Registered Apprentices** for the construction. Failing this reduces your 30% credit to just 6%.

DOL: IRA Labor Standards

The 5MW Size Cap

The **+10% Indian Land Bonus** is ONLY for projects under 5MW (Microreactors). SMRs and Large Reactors are ineligible for this specific bonus.

IRS: Rev. Proc. 2025-11 Size Constraints

Domestic Content

Starting in 2026, if you don't meet domestic steel/iron and component targets, your Direct Pay refund can be reduced (penalty) or disqualified.

Treasury: Domestic Content Requirements

Executive Research Summary

Based on recent consultations and OBBBA (2025) legislation, Tribal energy sovereignty is prioritized but size-constrained. Microreactors (< 5MW) are the most highly subsidized energy asset in US history, capable of capturing up to 70% of overnight capital in cash refunds. Utility-scale projects remain viable through the DOE Tribal Energy Financing Program which can cover up to 100% of remaining balance via low-interest loan guarantees.
