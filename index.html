Cash for Clean Energy:
A Guide for Tribal Nations

For the first time in history, Tribal Governments can receive multi-billion dollar cash refunds from the IRS to build advanced nuclear power plants. This portal explains how these laws work and allows you to model your project's specific savings.

New to Tribal Nuclear Benefits?

1

What is "Direct Pay"?

Normally, "Tax Credits" only help people who pay taxes. Under a new law called **Elective Pay (Direct Pay)**, the IRS will send a **cash check** directly to Tribal Governments for the value of their energy project—covering up to 70% of the cost.

Source: IRS Pub. 5817-G
2

The "5 Megawatt Line"

Small projects (**Microreactors** under 5 MW) get the biggest "bonus" credits for being on Indian Land. Larger projects (**SMRs**) don't get that specific bonus, but they get other massive discounts for using American-made steel.

Source: IRS Rev. Proc. 2025-11
3

The "Uranium Legacy"

If your land was used for historic uranium mining (96% of US defense mines were on Tribal Land), you likely qualify as an **"Energy Community"**. This gives your nuclear project an automatic **10% extra discount**.

Source: DOE Energy Community Map

How the "Stack" Works

Think of these benefits like building blocks. You start with a **30% Base Discount** (if you use fair labor rules). Then, you add blocks for things like:

  • +10% Using American-made parts (**Domestic Content**)
  • +10% Building on former mining/fossil sites (**Energy Community**)
  • +10% Siting on Tribal Land (**Indian Land Bonus** - Micro only)

Statutory Limit: The "Stack" is capped at a maximum of 70%.

Compliance Timeline & Critical Rules

2026 Windows

Competitive application window for the §48E(h) Indian Land bonus runs Feb 2 – Aug 7, 2026. Only 200MW is available.

No "Bad" Parts

Foreign Entity of Concern (FEOC) rules mean parts from Russia or China can disqualify your cash refund entirely starting in 2026.

The 2033 Deadline

Benefits stay at 100% value through 2033. Projects starting in 2034 see a drop to 75% value (phasedown).

Labor is Key

Failure to pay prevailing wages drops your base credit from 30% to just 6%. Documentation is mandatory.
